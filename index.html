Frequently asked questions

What is Shock Diamonds?

It’s an educational visualization of compressible gas flow that shows how Mach diamonds form in 3D. It is not a professional engineering or safety tool.

Does the app track me?

No. There is no tracking, analytics, or user account system.

Can I add my own 3D model?

Yes. You can import a custom 3D model to place into the flame visualization. See Custom 3D Models for tips.

Do you offer refunds or manage subscriptions here?

There are no in-app subscriptions or accounts. Purchases and refunds are handled by the App Store according to Apple’s policies.

Troubleshooting

Performance feels slow or device gets warm

  • Close other apps to free memory and GPU.
  • Reduce simulation detail or particle counts in settings, if available.
  • Ensure Low Power Mode is off for best performance.
  • Restart the device if it’s been a while since the last reboot.

The app crashes or freezes

  • Update to the latest version of Shock Diamonds and iOS/iPadOS.
  • Try a device restart. If it persists, email us with steps to reproduce.

My model imports but looks too big/small or is misplaced

  • Check your model’s scale units in your 3D tool (meters vs. centimeters).
  • Reset the model’s transforms (apply scale/rotation) before exporting.
  • Center the model near the origin (0,0,0) before export.

Import fails

  • Confirm the format is supported by the current app version (see in-app import screen).
  • Try re-exporting with embedded textures/materials.
  • Use a simpler mesh (fewer polygons) and remove non-manifold geometry.

Custom 3D models

Shock Diamonds lets you place a user-provided 3D model into the visualization.

  • How to import: Use the app’s “Import Model” flow (e.g., from Files). Follow on-screen prompts.
  • Supported formats: See the in-app import screen for the authoritative list for your version.
  • Tips for best results:
    • Keep polygon count reasonable for mobile GPUs.
    • Apply transforms and ensure scale is consistent.
    • Prefer a single, watertight mesh and embedded textures.
